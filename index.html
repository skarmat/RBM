A Secret My Grandmother Told Me About the Red Moon

When I was your age, living under the vast night sky in Nubra, I remember one evening seeing my grandmother with a little sadness on her face. She was lighting a butter lamp, a Chod-mey, and her lips were moving in silent prayer. She took a deep, heavy breath, and I knew something was wrong. I went to her and asked, "Ama-ley, what happened? Why all this prayer?" Instead of answering, she guided me to the window and pointed to the sky. The bright, full Moon was fading, turning into a deep, red orb. "The Moon is in trouble," she whispered. "And I am praying for its safety. Let me tell you why..."

She told me that long, long ago, there were hungry demons who lived on the Earth, causing trouble wherever they went. They were so fierce that the world was out of balance. Finally, the great master Guru Rinpoche decided to confront them. Using his immense wisdom, he subdued the demons and, instead of destroying them, he cast them into the heavens, binding them to the sky so they could never again trouble the world of humans.

"But," the elder said, his eyes twinkling in the dim light, "he couldn't take away their hunger. So now, exiled in the sky, they sometimes get so desperately hungry that they try to take a bite out of the Sun or the Moon."

"What about the Moon?" I asked. She pointed up at the glowing red Moon. "Look," She whispered. "That is what you see now. The demons are trying to swallow the Moon. That red color is the Moon struggling in their shadowy jaws!"

"The demons are trying to swallow the Moon," I echoed. "But that Seeing I was a bit worried, She quickly added the most important part. "But they never succeed. Their power is limited, and they can only take a small bite. The Moon always breaks free and shines even more brilliantly afterward, to show that it has won."
From that day on, a lunar eclipse was never just a scientific event for me. It was a live story playing out across the cosmos‚Äîa powerful reminder that even when things look their darkest, light and courage will always return.

What is a Total Lunar Eclipse?

Imagine you're playing a game of tag in a sunny field. If someone runs behind you, they'll be in your shadow, right? A lunar eclipse is something similar, but on a cosmic scale! A total lunar eclipse happens when the Sun, Earth, and Moon line up perfectly, with the Earth in the middle. The Earth blocks the Sun's light from reaching the Moon, casting its giant shadow over it.

The Science of the Red Glow

Why does the Moon turn red instead of disappearing? The answer is Earth's atmosphere. As sunlight passes through our atmosphere, it acts like a lens, bending red light towards the Moon while scattering away most of the blue light. Think of it as the ultimate sunset, but projected onto the lunar surface.

Just like how the sun's light turns red during a sunrise or sunset, Earth's atmosphere bends the light onto the Moon during an eclipse. Image credit: lifeontheplanetladakh.com

How to Watch

The best part about a lunar eclipse is that you don't need any special equipment to watch it! Unlike a solar eclipse, a lunar eclipse is perfectly safe to look at with your naked eyes. Here are some tips for the best viewing experience: Find a comfortable spot away from bright city lights. Give your eyes a few minutes to adjust to the dark. You can use binoculars or a telescope to get a closer look at the craters and seas of the red Moon! Invite your friends and family to watch with you!
